Scientists at NASA were left speechless this morning after a rogue pigeon accidentally activated an experimental weather-control satellite, causing it to snow spaghetti across parts of southern Europe for nearly three hours.

The incident reportedly began at exactly 4:17 a.m. when the pigeon, identified by internet users as “Greg,” flew into a restricted launch facility through an open maintenance hatch. According to leaked internal documents, the bird landed directly on a control panel connected to Project SkyFork — a top-secret atmospheric experiment allegedly designed to influence cloud formation during extreme climate emergencies.

Instead of stabilizing weather conditions, however, the satellite malfunctioned and released what witnesses described as “thousands of noodles descending from the sky like soft icy rain.”

Residents across small towns in Italy and France shared videos online showing streets covered in spaghetti, traffic halted by slippery pasta accumulation, and confused pigeons aggressively defending piles of parmesan packets that mysteriously appeared alongside the noodles.

Local authorities initially believed the event was a bizarre food-processing accident. One mayor reportedly warned citizens not to eat the sky pasta “until its cheese composition can be verified.” Despite the warning, several teenagers were seen collecting the spaghetti in buckets, with one claiming it was “surprisingly al dente.”

Meanwhile, conspiracy forums exploded with theories suggesting the event was:

* a secret carb-based population control experiment,
* an attempt to distract people from alien activity,
* or a marketing campaign by a major pasta company that “went too far.”

A spokesperson for Barilla denied any involvement, although internet users pointed out that the company’s social media account posted the phrase “the future is saucy” just hours before the incident.

Environmental scientists are now studying the long-term ecological consequences of “high-altitude noodle precipitation.” Early concerns include ducks becoming unusually aggressive, city drains clogging with pesto residue, and one extremely determined raccoon that reportedly dragged twelve kilograms of linguine into a sewer tunnel before disappearing entirely.

As for Greg the pigeon, officials say he has not been located. Social media users, however, have already declared him “the carb commander” and created fan pages demanding diplomatic immunity for the bird.
